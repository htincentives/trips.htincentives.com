Nestled high in the Canadian Rockies, the town of Banff is located within the Banff National Park. Banff was incorporated as a municipality in 1990 and has a population of around 8,000 permanent residents. The town site covers 1.52 square miles and is at an elevation of 4,537 feet, making it the highest town in Canada. Banff is flanked on all sides by towering mountains, including Mount Rundle, Sulphur Mountain, Mount Norquay, and Cascade Mountain. More than 4,000,000 visitors travel to Banff National Park every year since 1996. The town of Banff is a spirited place, alive with adventure and wonder.
